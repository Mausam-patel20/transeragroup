A

Tech-Illustrated Current Direction

Custom SVG illustration · Sans-serif throughout · Floating info cards · Gold gradient accent

Posture

Modern infrastructure-tech firm. Professional but approachable. Closer in feel to a SaaS or fintech company than a traditional consultancy.

What it does well

  • Distinctive — won't be confused with WSP or Stantec
  • Reliable rendering — no broken-image risk
  • Floating data cards signal "we measure things"
  • Friendly enough for SME audience

What it gives up

  • Reads as "designed" rather than "real"
  • Illustration may feel less authoritative for utility / municipal buyers
  • Less editorial gravitas than Tier-1 consulting sites

B

Photographic Realism More "consultancy" feel

Real-photo placeholder · Sans-serif headline with gold underline · Inline stat strip · Clean rectangular geometry

Posture

Established infrastructure consultancy. Closer to WSP, Arcadis, or Burns & McDonnell. Says "we are real, we are seasoned, we are not a startup."

What it does well

  • Photographic realism builds trust faster
  • Better fit for utility / municipal procurement teams
  • Inline stats are more confident than cards
  • Reads "professional services" not "tech product"

What it gives up

  • Requires sourcing real, on-brand photography (~$200–$800)
  • Less distinctive — could be confused with larger firms
  • Stock photography risk: looks generic if poorly chosen

How to give me notes that actually help.

Tomorrow morning, sit with the three variants above and the benchmark sites you mentioned (WSP, Arcadis, Stantec, CIMA+). Don't try to redesign — just react. Type your answers as quickly as you can; instinct is more useful than analysis.

  1. Of A, B, C — which one would your most demanding prospect (a municipal procurement officer or fleet director) take most seriously? Why?
  2. Which one feels most "like Kapil" — the actual person and how you want to show up? Why?
  3. Looking at WSP / Arcadis / Stantec next to these three — which variant holds its own, and which feels weaker by comparison?
  4. Is there a fourth direction you keep wanting that none of these capture? Describe it in one sentence.
  5. Which variant could you imagine still loving in three years?
  6. Are there any specific elements you want to mix — e.g., "Variant C's headline + Variant B's stat strip + Variant A's data cards"? Be specific.
  7. For the sections below the hero (services, approach, industries, speaking) — do you want them to follow the same posture as the hero, or shift register?
